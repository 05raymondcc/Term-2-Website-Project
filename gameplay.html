First of all, all the modes that exist in osugame are different. For example, as mentioned in overview, there are four separate modes with different mechanics. The four titles below demonstrate the mechanics of the games and how to play them; including some information regarding the history of the game. Although the four modes vary in a variety of stuff, the playstyle of each one of them is incredibly close. All you need is a keyboard and mouse to play those games.


osu!standard

The most popular version of osu and the default version. There are tons of unknown mechanics that are so many times different from the other rhythm games. A regular person with no experience to this type of game(rhythm games) would be hard to elucidate, therefore I will explain the mechanics right here.

Circle Approach Rate

How fast the line(green line in the picture) approaches to the circle, when the line hits the circle, you have to tap it. The accuracy is determined in three ways, precision, aim and UR(Unstable Rate). UR is how shaky you are when clicking the circle. The four possible accuracy points you can achieve are: MISS, 50, 100, 300. With 300 being the best, and MISS obviously being the worst.




Slider

To score a 300 on a slider, all you have to do is to hover your cursor over the circle, and when the line hits the circle, you can drag your cursor as the slider ball in the cursor moves. A 100 will be given if you hit the cursor but have not followed the line accurately. Last but not least, a MISS will be achieved when you don't hover and aim on anything.





osu!taiko

This version of osu is out of the japanese game "taiko" but on a computer instead of playing it on a drums with your red and blue sticks, you tap the keys on your keyboard "Z" and "X" to hit the notes that are coming out of the computer. The mechanics of this game are pretty simple and should be easy to understand, therefore no need to explain it.





osu!mania

This version of osu is like the mobile game Arcaea, a rhythm game where you click tiles that appears on the screen fast. In addition, this game requires lots of skill in terms of being able to function on different keys accurately with precision by tapping not only "Z" and "X". Please click the "Show" button for more information or a introductory mania walkthrough.

The Proper Bindings for Mania

First of all, you use a keyboard to play the game, only a keyboard. But then you have tons of keys you need to get used to tapping. Since there are usually 7 different roads the keys could come from, you need 7 different keys to destroy the note when it reaches you. The 7 different keys should be placed in a vertical order. For example, I use ZXCVBNM which are all aligned to each other horizontally.








osu!ctb

Also known as catchthebeat, this is one of the most popular japanese minigames since the 20th centuary. osu!ctb is a budget version of the real japanese catchthebeat, you are supposed to catch fruits or notes falling from the sky. Overall an easy game to understand, but when mods(stuff that make the game harder) are inputed, it does become challenging.
